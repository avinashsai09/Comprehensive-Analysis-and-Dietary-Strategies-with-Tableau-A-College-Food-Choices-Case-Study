Problem Statement

Universities need evidence-backed visibility into student eating habits so they can design practical wellness interventions. This project uses Tableau to identify patterns in breakfast consumption, comfort food reasons, calorie awareness, exercise, cuisine preferences, and health perception.

Key Insights

Breakfast Supports Focus

Regular breakfast consumption is associated with stronger perceived health and academic readiness.

Stress Drives Comfort Food

Boredom, sadness, and stress are dominant comfort-food triggers, especially during demanding academic periods.

Healthier Cafeteria Choices Matter

Low fruit and vegetable intake paired with frequent fast-food habits suggests a need for convenient balanced meal options.
