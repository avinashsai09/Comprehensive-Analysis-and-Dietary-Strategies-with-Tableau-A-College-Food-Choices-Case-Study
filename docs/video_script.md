# 5-7 Minute Video Script

## Opening

Hello, my name is [Your Name], and this project is titled Comprehensive Analysis and Dietary Strategies with Tableau: A College Food Choices Case Study.

## Problem Statement

The main problem is that college students often struggle with irregular eating habits because of academic pressure, busy schedules, stress, and limited healthy food options. This project analyzes food choices and lifestyle factors to understand how they relate to health perception and academic focus.

## Dataset

The dataset contains 120 student records and 41 variables. It includes gender, GPA, grade level, breakfast habits, calorie awareness, fruit and vegetable intake, vitamin consumption, exercise frequency, eating out, cooking frequency, comfort-food reasons, cuisine preferences, healthy feeling, and life rewarding scores.

## Data Preprocessing

Before visualization, the dataset was checked for missing values, inconsistent labels, and incorrect data types. Categorical fields such as gender, breakfast, comfort-food reasons, and health category were standardized. Numeric survey fields were prepared as measures or dimensions depending on the visualization requirement.

## Tableau Dashboard

The Tableau dashboard begins with a KPI summary card showing total records, average GPA, average weight, and average grade level. The breakfast chart compares breakfast categories. The eating out versus cooking chart shows lifestyle behavior. Health category by gender compares healthy and unhealthy classifications. The vitamins and fruit or vegetable view uses a parameter to switch between selected consumption measures. The food-selection parameter allows comparison across cuisine preference scores.

## Tableau Story

The story guides the viewer through the analysis. The first story point highlights comfort-food reasons and shows that boredom, sadness, and stress are important triggers. The next story points connect food routines, health category, life rewarding scores, and the final dashboard summary.

## Key Insights

The analysis suggests that students with more regular breakfast and balanced food habits tend to show better health and academic focus indicators. Comfort-food eating is often linked with emotional states such as boredom, stress, and sadness. Fruit and vegetable intake can be improved, and frequent eating out suggests that cafeteria interventions could make a strong difference.

## Recommendations

For students, the recommendation is to adopt quick breakfast options, reduce late-night junk food, and use healthier stress-management alternatives such as fruits, home-cooked meals, and exercise. For the cafeteria, recommendations include fresh salad bars, balanced meal combos, healthier snacks, and clearly labeled nutritious meals.

## Closing

To conclude, this project shows how Tableau can turn raw student food-choice data into meaningful insights. The dashboard and story can help universities, counselors, and cafeteria managers design better health and wellness strategies for students. Thank you.
